# FDM 3D Printer Common Problem Troubleshooting Guide 
Use this guide to help you identify and solve the most common issues that may occur while 3D printing.     
 ![](1.png)

When printing, unexpected challenges may arise that will need to be resolved in order to complete a successful print.  Often times, the appropriate response to a printing issue is not immediately apparent.  This guide outlines some of the most common issues faced by 3D printing practitioners and highlights steps that may be taken to resolve those issues.

## <a id = "issue16"></a> Blobs and/or pimples on exterior surface
<table><tbody><tr><td><p><a id="Issue17"></a>Issue/Title</p></td><td><p>Blobs and/or pimples on exterior surface</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Example Image / Graphic</p></td><td><p><img alt="" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/ZNXu24AZ2DUTIHS7Tcmwqb3p60c_t95md__nm6y8fJC1IYVx8rE5clqjXoifMAMJpNUD6_tXaD_6OIkPHilH4GWLpH-mmg20LxlPRbY=s575"></p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Issue Details</p></td><td>&nbsp;The exterior surface of the print has small, unwanted extrusions or bumps, often referred to as blobs or pimples. These can occur randomly or at consistent intervals, disrupting the smooth finish of the print. They can vary in size and are usually more noticeable on the surface of the print.</td></tr><tr><td><p>Cause 1</p></td><td><p>&nbsp;Incorrect retraction settings and/or temperature fluctuations</p><p>Blobs and pimples often occur when the printer retracts the filament too slowly or not far enough, causing excess filament to leak out of the nozzle. This can also be caused by inconsistent temperature control, where the heat from the extruder causes the filament to ooze out in unwanted areas.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Suggested Solutions</p></td><td><ol><li><p>Clean nozzle</p></li><li>Adjust the retraction length and speed</li><li><p>Check connection (USB)</p></li><li><p>Pauses, try printing from SD</p></li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>

## <a id = "issue17"></a> Weak infill
<table><tbody><tr><td><p><a id="Issue18"></a>Issue/Title</p></td><td><p>Weak infill</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Example Image / Graphic</p></td><td><p><img alt="" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/HwT755jHBP4_vmTsSTkBdpimazW1p3b1wonRbl985q212bZfNIS9YJbDOGUE22LX6E9q5EVGhsCF8XVi0U8HO85Ka8j7Aez4qma8qyM=s576"></p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Issue Details</p></td><td>&nbsp;The infill of the print, which provides internal support, is not strong or dense enough. This may result in a print that is weak, flimsy, or more susceptible to breakage. Additionally, a print temperature that is too low can lead to poor layer adhesion, resulting in a weak infill structure.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Suggested Solutions 1</p></td><td><ol><li><p>Change infill pattern</p></li><li><p>Lower print speed</p></li><li><p>Increase infill extrusion width</p></li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>

## <a id = "issue18"></a> Stops extruding in the middle of a print
<table><tbody><tr><td><p><a id="Issue19"></a>Issue/Title</p></td><td><p>Stops extruding in the middle of a print</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Example Image / Graphic</p></td><td><p><img alt="" src="https://lh3.googleusercontent.com/Omj-GaeZHGhNkPYNXSf26kMopo41WIwWJPJSA0WeBBRNyAuW-_L6Ilu6euN6-odNNWVdeXPKiGfWlcelql_CG3uOWljDjZZWzAHw0Q=s573"></p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Issue Details</p></td><td>The printer abruptly stops extruding filament during the print process. This can result in incomplete prints, with layers missing from the point of stoppage. The issue can occur at any point during the print and can affect any part of the model being printed.</td></tr><tr><td><p>Cause 1</p></td><td>&nbsp;<p>Filament feed issues and/or extruder hardware problems</p><p>The printer may stop extruding if there are issues with the filament feed, such as the filament running out, getting tangled, or being stripped. Additionally, hardware problems like a jammed nozzle or heat creep (where heat travels up the extruder, causing the filament to soften and jam before it reaches the nozzle) can also cause the printer to stop extruding.</p></td></tr><tr><td><p>Suggested Solutions 1</p></td><td><ol><li><p>Out of filament</p></li><li><p>Filament tangle before extruder</p></li><li><p>Stripped filament</p></li><li><p>Nozzle jam</p></li><li><p>Heat creep</p></li></ol></td></tr></tbody></table>
